Difference between BORG Nonntal and IIS Olivetti

Grades

Italian grades are distributed on a decimal scale (1-10) and 10 is the maximum number that can be achieved, whereas Austrian grades range from 1 to 5 with maximum grade 1.

Difference in length of lessons

In the Ivrean School lessons last 48 minutes, while in Salzburg the lessons last 50 minutes.

Breaks

The intervals in the Ivrean School are two, which last ten minutes each and occur every two modules, unlike in the Austrian School there are 5 intervals whose durations are: 5 minutes, 10 minutes, 15 minutes, 5 minutes, 5 minutes. Lunch breaks in the Italian school last 48 minutes, while those in the Austrian school last 50 minutes.

High school route duration

The Italian school route has a duration of 5 years, unlike the Austrian school route which has a duration of 4 years.

School day duration

The Ivrean school provides only one afternoon during the week until 3:30 pm, three days classes end at 1:10 pm, and the fifth day lasts until 12:20 pm, while the school in Salzburg provides three afternoons per week that last until 4:15 pm, and the remaining two days end at 1:40 pm.

Footwear

The only footwear allowed in the school in Salzburg is shoes or slippers used in the school building, in the Ivrean school only shoes can be worn, which are also used outdoor.

Language subjects

At the school in Salzburg, students study three languages: German, English and Italian/French/Latin, unlike those in Ivrea whose only language subjects are Italian and English.

Students

There are about 1200 students in the Ivrean school and only about 400 in the Austrian school.
